# KOVA Main Website V1 Blueprint

## Core Goal
- Make KOVA look legitimate, sharp, and established.
- Show teams and momentum.
- Give people a clear path to explore and apply.
- Feel premium, not overloaded.

## Identity
- The site should be an esports org homepage first.
- It should show off KOVA's teams and highlights.
- Recruitment should not dominate the experience.
- The apply flow should be present as a clean bridge into the apply website.

## Visual Direction
- Clean elite.
- Dark luxury premium.
- Black and charcoal as the main base.
- White typography for clarity and contrast.
- Restrained gold and silver accents.
- Possibly one subtle secondary accent tone if it helps the design.
- No cluttered neon esports style.
- No generic SaaS look.

## Homepage Story
### 1. Hero
- Big brand-first opening.
- Strong KOVA headline.
- Short subline about what KOVA is.
- Primary CTA: `View Teams`
- Secondary CTA: `Apply`
- Background should feel alive, not flat.

### 2. Legitimacy Signal
- A compact proof strip right below the hero.
- Can show:
  - active rosters
  - events played
  - current season focus
  - featured region or title

### 3. Team Showcase
- One of the most important sections.
- Present KOVA teams and divisions clearly.
- Likely includes:
  - main roster
  - academy
  - premier
  - tournament roster or special squads
- Cards should feel premium, not like raw database entries.

### 4. Highlights / Momentum
- Featured results.
- Featured players.
- Recent wins.
- Spotlight stats.
- Manual or dummy data is fine for V1 if the presentation feels alive.

### 5. Featured Event / Match Focus
- A section for:
  - next event
  - featured match
  - standings snapshot
  - tournament spotlight
- This is the natural place for future Tracker-style integrations.

### 6. Join KOVA
- Short and clean.
- Not a massive recruiting block.
- More of a bridge:
  - want to play, support, or work with KOVA?
  - learn how to apply
- CTA should lead into the apply website.

### 7. Social / Community / Footer
- Social links.
- Optional community mention.
- Footer links and legal structure later if needed.

## Navigation
- Home
- Teams
- Highlights
- Events
- Apply

Optional:
- About

## Pages For V1
### Home
- Full homepage story.

### Teams
- Proper team showcase page.
- Divisions and rosters.
- Player cards or placeholders.
- Expandable later into deeper team pages.

### Highlights
- Featured moments.
- Recent activity.
- Spotlight results.
- Manual or curated content is fine for V1.

### Events
- Upcoming tournaments.
- Recent events.
- Featured matches.
- Manual or dummy content is fine for V1.

### Apply
- A bridge page or direct CTA into `apply-web`.
- Should explain where to apply without rebuilding the full application system here.

## Content Model For V1
Manual or dummy content is fine for:
- team cards
- highlights
- featured match or event
- stats strip
- partner placeholders if used

Should stay dynamic-ready later for:
- standings
- player performance
- tracker data
- schedules
- tournament states

## Important Design Rules
- The hero must feel expensive.
- The team section must feel like the heart of the org.
- Highlights must make the site feel active.
- Apply should be present, but not dominate the site.
- Every section should feel intentional, not like filler.

## What Can Wait For V2
- Tracker integration
- live standings
- player detail pages
- news or articles
- automated result feeds
- deeper event management
- public partner system if wanted

## Best Build Order
1. Home
2. Teams
3. Events and Highlights structure
4. Apply bridge
5. Later dynamic integrations

## Summary
KOVA Main Web V1 should feel like a real esports brand homepage first: premium, active, sharp, and public-facing. It should build legitimacy through presentation, team visibility, and momentum without becoming too data-heavy too early.
